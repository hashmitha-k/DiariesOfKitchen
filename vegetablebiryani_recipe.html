Vegetable Biryani

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potatoes)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • Fresh cilantro and mint leaves for garnish
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté Whole Spices: Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods. Sauté until aromatic.
  3. Cook Onions: Add the sliced onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add Aromatics: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and the slit green chili. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add the chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Incorporate Vegetables: Add the mixed vegetables to the pan. Stir well to coat them with the spice mixture and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add Yogurt: Lower the heat and add the yogurt. Mix thoroughly and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  8. Combine Rice and Water: Add the soaked and drained rice to the pan. Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt to taste. Stir gently to combine all ingredients.
  9. Cook the Biryani: Bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  10. Finish and Serve: Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest, covered, for 5 minutes. Before serving, fluff the rice gently with a fork, drizzle with lemon juice, and garnish with fresh cilantro and mint leaves.

Diet Plan & Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 plate

  • Calories: Approximately 300 kcal
  • Fat: 10g
  • Carbohydrates: 45g
  • Protein: 6g
  • Sugar: 5g

Diet Tips:

  • For a low-fat version: Use less oil or opt for a light cooking spray.
  • For a vegan version: Substitute yogurt with plant-based yogurt or coconut milk.
