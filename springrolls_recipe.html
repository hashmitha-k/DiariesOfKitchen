Spring Rolls

Ingredients

  • 8-10 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup cooked shrimp or tofu (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice noodles (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce (for dipping)
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter (for dipping)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (for dipping)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (for dipping)
  • 1 tablespoon water (for dipping)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Filling: In a bowl, combine shredded lettuce, carrots, cucumber, cilantro, and mint leaves. If using shrimp or tofu, slice into small pieces. If using rice noodles, cook them according to package instructions.
  2. Soak Rice Paper Wrappers: Fill a shallow bowl with warm water. Gently dip each rice paper wrapper into the water for 10-15 seconds until soft, then lay it flat on a clean, dry surface.
  3. Assemble the Rolls: Place a small handful of the filling mixture in the center of the rice paper. Add the shrimp or tofu (optional) and rice noodles (optional). Fold the sides of the rice paper over the filling, then roll it tightly from the bottom up.
  4. Repeat: Continue making spring rolls with the remaining wrappers and filling ingredients.
  5. Make Dipping Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together hoisin sauce, peanut butter, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and water to make the dipping sauce.
  6. Serve: Serve the spring rolls with the dipping sauce on the side. Enjoy!

Diet Plan & Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 spring roll

  • Calories: 80 kcal
  • Fat: 2g
  • Carbohydrates: 14g
  • Protein: 3g
  • Sugar: 3g

Diet Tips:

  • For a low-calorie version: Skip the dipping sauce or use a low-sodium soy sauce and peanut butter.
  • For a vegan version: Use tofu instead of shrimp and ensure your dipping sauce is vegan-friendly.

Dietary Information

  • Gluten-Free: Yes (ensure rice paper wrappers are gluten-free)
  • Vegetarian: Yes (if using tofu)
  • Contains Dairy: No
  • Contains Eggs: No
  • Vegan: Yes (if using tofu and vegan-friendly dipping sauce)
